Article HTML

October 28, 2025

Reading the article, “What is Semantic HTML? And How to Use It Correctly?” helped me understand how to organize my code when designing specific elements. It also gave me a clearer idea of how semantic HTML improves user experience and makes websites easier to navigate. After reading, I began paying closer attention to nesting elements to ensure my layout remained clean and easy to read.

I used tags like the footer so users can easily go back to the top or return to the home page. I have also included my contact information for anyone who would like to reach out. The nav and article tags helped users find certain blog posts or topics they might want to revisit, while the section tags separated different types of content. Using these elements helped me keep the structure organized and made the content flow more naturally.

Each element plays a part in showing how the page connects. The nav helps users move around the site, section divides the content, article highlights each post, and the footer ties everything together. Overall, it keeps the website visually balanced and easy to follow.

For my article’s styling, I wanted to create a calm and comfortable atmosphere for readers. Since my content is more like a journal or blog post, I styled my website to feel like a personal journal entry. I experimented with handwritten-style fonts to give it a more authentic and personal feel, and I used soft, muted colors to make the design soothing to look at. The hardest part was figuring out how I wanted users to feel when reading my site. It took time to find fonts and widths that matched my vision but were still readable. In the end, I achieved a design that reflects my creativity while creating a relaxing experience for users.
