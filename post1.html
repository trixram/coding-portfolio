History of the Internet

October 21, 2025

As a college student, streaming services are the cheapest option we have to watch our favorite shows like Love Island or The Summer I Turned Pretty, compared to paying monthly for cable and having to prioritize a specific time to watch them.

However, streaming services have increased from about $50 to almost $70 per month as companies claim this is to improve the app experience and expand their content libraries. With these price hikes, many consumers have chosen to boycott certain companies, similar to what has happened in other industries since the 2020 pandemic.

Even though streaming makes it easier to access shows and movies for one price, being able to afford this luxury as a student comes with its challenges. With the changing prices of Netflix, Hulu, and Peacock, I find it difficult to decide which streaming service can give me entertainment while still keeping my budget under control.

Despite the many advertisements for student discounts, affordability remains an issue because these discounts disappear after graduation, leading to more turnover in subscriptions. From a corporate standpoint, offering discounted prices is a smart tactic to attract new customers, but it also shows the disadvantages of rising standard prices.
